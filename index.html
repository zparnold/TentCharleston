About

Covering the Holy City with the Gospel of Jesus Christ

What

Tent Charleston is a two week evangelistic campaign to proclaim the gospel of Jesus Christ in the open air to the city of Charleston.

Where

Corner of Meeting and Lee Streets in downtown Charleston – Look for the big tent!

When

Nightly at 7pm from Sept 12 – Sept 27, 2015

** Sept 12 is a night of united prayer for the city

Schedule for Sept 12, 2015

7-10 pm | Night of repentance and prayer for the city. We will repent of our sin and rebellion against God, both individually and corporately, and we will ask the Lord to heal our hearts and heal our land. We will rend our hearts before the Lord and petition him for a mighty harvest here in Charleston and beyond. Please join us in prayer for our city.

Schedule from Sept 13 - 27, 2015

12-7 pm | Worship and prayer at the tent (corner of Meeting and Lee Streets)

2-5 pm | Street teams sent to various sites around the city, small healing prayer tents set up, hand out flyers for the evening tent campaign (meet at the tent)

7-10 pm | Evangelistic meeting at the tent with worship and the proclaimed Word of God



The Vision

Because Jesus Christ is the only hope for Charleston and for America. As a nation, we have turned away from the Lord in every realm of our culture and daily life. We have removed the Word of God from our schools and deprived our children of connecting with the Lord through prayer. We have removed the name of Jesus from the public square and replaced his law with our own, calling good evil and evil good. We trust in social services, mental health and law enforcement to fix our broken state, but they do not have the tools to deal with the spiritual root of our problems, and as a result they have healed the wounds of our people lightly. Only the gospel of Jesus Christ has the power to heal, save and transform lives.

We are living in the darkest day in American history. From the inception of our nation, the American people have turned to the church during times of moral crisis to show them the straight and narrow path of God, but even much of the church has turned away from the Lord and forsaken his righteous ways. The smooth messages that are being preached from pulpits across our land are tickling our ears rather than convicting our hearts and leading us further and further down a path of spiritual deception.

The time is now to return to the ancient path and to proclaim Jesus Christ and him crucified. What is the answer to the issues raging on in our nation and the world today? The gospel of Jesus Christ. The problems in America are not rooted in Washington, DC but in every one of our hearts. Until we repent of our sin and acknowledge our rebellion against God, true and lasting change will continue to elude us. There is only one way to community change and that is through individual heart change. And there is only one way to a new heart – the gospel of our dear Lord Jesus.

Why the tent? The tent is a visible sign of God’s love to the city of Charleston, a tangible reminder of a Heavenly Father who pursues his precious children right where they are, even in the streets. As people come to the church house less and less, it is critical that we go and meet them where they are. As we flood the streets with the invitation of the gospel, many who would never set foot in a church building will have a chance to hear and believe the good news of Jesus Christ – and be saved for eternity!

BECAUSE THE LOVE OF JESUS CHANGES HEARTS...AND CHANGED HEARTS CHANGE THE WORLD

Our team

Matthew Pridgen

Matthew Pridgen is the evangelist at St John’s Chapel on the east side of downtown Charleston under the leadership of Brother Dallas Wilson. He lives on the peninsula with his wife and three young boys, and his heartbeat is to see the city, nation and world transformed through the proclaimed gospel of Jesus Christ.

Jonathan Tremaine Thomas

Jonathan Tremaine Thomas is the founder and director of the Commission, a missions network committed to seeing Holy dwelling places established in the nations of the earth through prevailing prayer, unceasing worship and creative manifestation of the Gospel of the Kingdom.

In order for Tent Charleston to be a kingdom success, we need your help! We are looking for partners and laborers to join us for the harvest in Charleston and beyond. Please pray to the Lord of the harvest to send laborers and ask if you might be one. If you do feel called to labor with us, please read the areas of service listed below. We all have different giftings and our goal is to help you find a place where you can contribute and thrive in the Lord.

Areas of Service

Intercessors

We are looking for prayer warriors who will pray with us before, during and after Tent Charleston. We have time slots for intercession in the tent from 12-7pm during the days that meetings are held and prayer teams for the nightly meetings from 7-10pm. We will then need prayer to cover new believers who are added to the body during the outreach.

Sign Up Now


Evangelists

During the two weeks of tent meetings, we will be sending out evangelism teams daily into the city, from 2-5pm. These teams will target strategic locations around the city to share the good news, pray for people and to invite them back to the nightly meetings. No experience necessary, just a burning heart for Jesus!

Sign Up Now


Worshipers

All day in the tent during the two weeks of Tent Charleston, we will have worship teams offering “a continual sacrifice of praise to God” (Heb 13:15). We have time slots available from 12-7pm, which will be open to worshipers and worship teams who have a heart to worship the Lord in spirit and in truth. We will then have a united worship team at the nightly meetings to usher in the presence of God into the Tent of Meeting.

Sign Up Now


Follow up Team

As new believers are added to the Kingdom, we understand the importance of getting them plugged into a local church body to disciple them in their walk with the Lord. The Lord’s commission in Matthew 28 is not just to make disciples and baptize them, but to “teach them to observe all that I have commanded you.” We are looking for individuals and church partners who will follow up with new believers to provide a home where they can grow and mature in the Lord.
